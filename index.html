About Me

I am a physician-scientist with over 30 years of clinical, research, and academic experience spanning cardiology, biomedical engineering, neuroradiology, and public health. My career has taken me across Taiwan, Canada, China, France, and the United States, allowing me to contribute to medical practice at both bedside and bench levels.


My interdisciplinary background — combining an MD from National Taiwan University, a PhD in Biomedical Engineering, and an MPH from Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health — drives my passion for translating cutting-edge research into improved patient outcomes. I am particularly interested in applying deep learning and AI to cardiovascular and pulmonary disease diagnostics.

Plans & Vision

Future Directions

Research & Clinical Goals

My ongoing focus is on integrating artificial intelligence and deep learning with clinical cardiology and pulmonary medicine to improve early detection and treatment of cardiovascular and oncological diseases. I aim to continue translational research bridging computational methods and clinical practice, with particular emphasis on lung nodule growth prediction, federated learning for privacy-preserving medical AI, and cardiovascular intervention optimization.


I am actively exploring opportunities in international medical collaboration and academic medicine across Taiwan, Canada, and the Asia-Pacific region, with the goal of fostering cross-institutional research networks.

Top 5 Biomedical News This Week

Week of April 14–20, 2026  |  Curated from leading biomedical sources

NEWS 1

T Cells Supercharged Against Cancer

Scientists discovered that blocking the protein Ant2 forces T cells to rewire their energy metabolism — making them more powerful, resilient, and effective at identifying and destroying cancer cells. The breakthrough may lead to next-generation immunotherapy strategies.

NEWS 2

RNA Barcodes Map Brain Neuron Connections with Single-Synapse Precision

Researchers developed a cutting-edge technique using RNA "barcodes" to map synaptic connectivity among neurons. The method captures thousands of neural links at single-synapse resolution, opening new windows into brain circuit architecture and neurological disease.

NEWS 3

Reversible Non-Hormonal Male Contraception — A Breakthrough at Cornell

Scientists at Cornell University announced progress toward a safe, reversible, non-hormonal male contraceptive that completely halts sperm production without affecting hormone levels — a long-sought advance in reproductive medicine.

NEWS 4

Nanodisc Platform Reveals HIV & Ebola Viral Vulnerabilities

A novel nanodisc-based platform allows scientists to study viral membrane proteins in a near-native lipid environment, faithfully mimicking real viruses. The platform revealed how antibodies recognize HIV and Ebola envelope proteins, accelerating vaccine and therapeutic development.

NEWS 5

AI Microbiome Analysis Detects Colorectal Cancer — No Colonoscopy Needed

Researchers used AI-driven gut microbiome mapping to identify subtle microbial signatures linked to colorectal cancer — without requiring invasive colonoscopy. This non-invasive diagnostic approach could transform cancer screening, particularly in resource-limited settings.
